Upma Recipe

Calories: 192

Contains: Protein, Fat

Ingredients

  • 1 cup semolina (rava)
  • 1/4 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add cumin seeds, urad dal, and chana dal.
  2. Add curry leaves, chopped green chili, and chopped onion. Sauté until the onion turns translucent.
  3. Add mixed vegetables and sauté for a few minutes.
  4. Add semolina (rava) and roast until it turns golden and aromatic.
  5. Boil water separately and add it gradually to the roasted rava while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat until the upma is cooked and the water is absorbed.
  7. Season with salt and garnish with chopped cilantro.
  8. Serve hot with coconut chutney or pickle.

Embark on a flavor-filled journey with Upma, a traditional South Indian dish that has earned its place as a beloved breakfast option across the country. Crafted from semolina and an array of aromatic spices, Upma is a savory delight that caters to both taste and texture enthusiasts. The process begins by roasting the semolina to golden perfection, filling the air with a comforting aroma that sets the stage for a culinary masterpiece. Mixed vegetables and a medley of spices are then combined with the roasted semolina, infusing each bite with vibrant flavors. The result is a dish that's not just visually appealing but also a celebration of South Indian culinary expertise. Upma can be enjoyed as a stand-alone breakfast or paired with coconut chutney, pickle, or yogurt. Its versatility and delectable taste make it a favorite that's equally cherished by families at home and food enthusiasts around the globe.
